Planning a Trip to Delhi

This is a document to help interested travelers plan their trip to Delhi!

Cultural Bazaar

Dastakar

A cultural bazaar near the border between Delhi and Gurgaon, Dastakar Delhi features a curated list of artisans from across India in a series of themed events that typically last a few weeks. If you are exploring Delhi during the springtime, be sure to visit their Holi and Basant festivals to enjoy exploring the colorful realm of traditional handicrafts!

Dilli Haat

Where Dastkar operates by event (Meaning it is not open to visitors all year round), Dilli Haat is a similar shopping area featuring tradional handicrafts. Although they often invite new artisans because of themed events, this market is open throughout the year (And you can bargain prices here).

Warning: Travellers visiting these places during the summer months, please be sure to wear clothes that keep you cool.

Monuments

Qutub Minar

The world's tallest brick minaret and a UNESCO world heritage site that was built by Qutubuddin Aibaq ( A sultan during Delhi's sultanate period that lasted from about 1206 A.D. to 1526 A.D). The minaret is surrounded by the ruins of Lal Kot, one of the major cities during this time period. Be sure to prebook tickets and travel by auto-rickshaw (Also known as tuk-tuk) as the crowds make it difficult to find parking nearby.

Humayun's Tomb

Humayun's Tomb is the resting place of one of the first Mughal emperors. The tomb, which was built using red sandstone and black and white marble, is believed to have been designed by his son, Jalaluddin Akbar. The tomb is also believed to have been one of the primary inspirations for the design of the Taj Mahal (That was designed by Akbar's grandson- Shah Jahan) as it looks like a red Taj Mahal.

Safdarjung's Tomb

Safdarjung's Tomb is situated in New Delhi and features the perfect example of the later styles of Mughal Architecture. The boundary walls and tomb display intricate carvings and artwork from the latter part of the Mughal Era.

Gardens

Lodhi Gardens

Lodhi Gardens is where most of the sultans from the Lodhi dynasty are buried (Again, from Delhi's sultanate period). The surrounding gardens are a popular picnic spot as people enjoy the nicer weather. The garden also features a popular duck pond (That recently introduced a shelter for the ducks and ducklings) and peacocks.

Sunder Nursery

Sunder Nursery was a former plant nursery that was set up to propogate plants for the city of Delhi. The area was later landscaped to resemble a Mughal garden and opened to the general public. Sunder nursery is also Delhi's first arboretum featuring over 300 types of trees!

Packing List

Essentials

  1. Passport and travel documents (Make sure to bring a print-out of your eVisa)
  2. Wallet
  3. T-shirts (I would recommend packing short sleeved T-shirts even in the wintertime as the weather can change at random)
  4. Shorts (If travelling in the summertime/springtime/monsoon)
  5. Longer pants (Jeans if travelling in the cooler months and cotton or linen pants if travelling in the warmer months)
  6. Sweaters/ sweatshirts (Yes, Delhi does have a winter- and it can get pretty cold without appropriate winterwear)
  7. Slippers
  8. Sneakers
  9. Medications- specifically, fever medication, anti-allergy medication and stomach medication (You will be able to find these things, but it's best to carry medications you are familiar with)
  10. Mosquito Repellent
  11. Toiletries
  12. Sunscreen
  13. Sunglasses
  14. Something to cover your head with (To protect your face from the sun)
  15. An umbrella

Not Essential, But Nice to Have

  • A reusable water-bottle
  • A light jacket (during the springtime and during Diwali season)
  • A cross-body bag
  • A handheld mist fan (You may get some funny looks, but this is a game-changer during the hot summer and spring months!)
