Academic Paper on Legislation in Old English

This paper examines the use of the word unrihte in Beowulf by considering its use as legal terminology. The paper explores whether the use of the word unrihte refers to a legal wrong, or whether the use of this word points to the beginnings of religious morality in Anglo-Saxon England ( in other words, how is the poet measuring 'rightness' in this portion of the text?).
